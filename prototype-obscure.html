Obscure

/əbˈskjʊr/
adj. v. CEFR C1 WAD 3.5 WAP 3.0

核心意象 Core image

由于"遮蔽"或"光线不足"而导致的模糊不清。事物本身并非不存在，而是被一层阴影、云雾或障碍物挡住了——导致观察者难以看清、难以理解或难以发现。

词根词源 Etymology

拉丁语 obscurus (dark, dusky, indistinct)，由 ob- (over, against, covering) + -scurus (covered by clouds) 构成。 可能与 scutum (shield 盾牌) 同源。

物理：昏暗/被遮挡 → 认知：晦涩难懂 → 社会：默默无闻

A thick fog rolls into the city. The skyscrapers, which are usually distinct, become faint outlines. The clouds physically come between your eyes and the buildings, making them hard to distinguish.

The mountains were obscured by a thick layer of morning mist.

A student tries to read a philosophy book from the 18th century. The language is archaic, the references are outdated, and the meaning is buried under layers of complex metaphors. It is not plain; it is hidden in darkness.

The author's point was obscured by unnecessarily complex jargon.

A painter lived in a small village and never sold a painting during his life. He died unknown to the world. He lived in the "shadows" of society, away from the spotlight of fame.

She was an obscure novelist until her third book unexpectedly became a bestseller.

词 核心区别 情感 / 语域 典型场景
Obscure 因被遮蔽而难懂，或因处于边缘而无人知晓 阴暗、冷门、学术 晦涩的诗歌、无闻的作家、云遮住山
Vague 轮廓不清，缺乏细节——像是没对好焦 疏忽、不确定 含糊的回答、模糊的记忆
Ambiguous 清晰但不确定，有两个或以上的解释 逻辑、双关 模棱两可的话、双关语
Opaque 完全阻断视线或理解，毫无穿透力 物理、厚重 不透明玻璃、完全读不懂的文章
Obscure vs Vague：Vague 是"没画好"（本身缺少细节）；Obscure 是"没照亮"（本身可能很复杂，但你看不到）。
Obscure vs Ambiguous：Ambiguous 是"向左还是向右？"（多选）；Obscure 是"前面是个啥？"（盲盒）。
